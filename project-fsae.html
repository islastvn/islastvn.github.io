UVic Formula Racing · P-02

FSAE Suspension
A-Arm Nodes

Overview

Design of aluminum suspension A-arm nodes for the University of Victoria EV Race car - the machined joints that connect the suspension arms to carbon fiber tubes. The aim was to make each node as light as possible while still safely hitting the kinetmatic constraints and carrying the loads the suspension sees on track.

What I did

  • Simulated max. suspension load cases using Dynatune, calculated bond-length with tear-out calculations, and tube-diameter with max. stress calculations.
  • Designed four aluminum A-arm nodes in SolidWorks, using FEA, topology optimization, while still meeting gemoetric constraints.
  • Achieved substantial mass reduction per component (in the range of 64–70%) while meeting the required safety factors.
  • Applied design-for-manufacturing principles to turn the optimized shapes into CNC-machinable aluminum parts bonded to carbon fiber tubes.

Focus

Structural design, simulation-driven optimization, lightweighting, and manufacturability for a competitive motorsport application.
