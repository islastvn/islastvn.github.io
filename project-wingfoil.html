Overview

A force measurement system built for wingfoil athletes, designed to capture how much force a rider applies through the handle during live water sessions. The goal was reliable, independent measurement of front and rear hand loads in a wet, salty, high-motion environment.

What I did

  • Designed a measurement setup using dual inline load cells mounted to a carbon fiber auxiliary boom, enabling independent front and rear hand force capture.
  • Developed a waterproof, salt-resistant electronics package built around an ESP32-S3 microcontroller with dual ADC amplifiers and a LiPo power system.
  • Housed the electronics in a sealed enclosure engineered for reliable operation in saltwater conditions.

Focus

Mechanical integration, waterproofing and sealing, embedded sensing, and reliable data capture in a demanding real-world setting.
